# Account Planning Database Design v0

## Description
This is the initial database design for the Account Planning application, which is an AI based sales & performance assistant for relationship managers in various industries. The application is designed to help relationship managers to improve their customer relationships and increase their sales based on data and AI generated insights and actions. Application is mostly suitable for multi-step actions based sales processes where relationship managers are responsible for a set of customers and have to proactively manage their relationships and increase their sales. The company sales strategy, corporate knowledge, sales quotas and realizations are all be bind to actionable insights and actions which will be generated, prioritized and tracked with the help of Artificial Intelligence, mostly LLMs. The application is designed to be industry agnostic, but with industry specific configurations and plugins.

!IMPORTANT: This is an AI first application. All the data structure should be designed with this in mind. This structure should also support modular architecture where new modules can be added or removed from the system. Some companies may not be ready for AI based insights and actions, so the system should be able to support a simple version of the application with basic features and gradually introduce more advanced features as the company becomes more comfortable with the system. 

## Main Entities
The main blocks and entities are:

- Company (tenant): application serves a multi-tenant architecture. Each company has its own set of users, roles, relations, and preferences.

- Products: each company has its own set of products. The product info mainly contains product specifications, product categories, and product hierarchy specific for each company. For example, 
    - In a bank, Revolving loan is a product, and its parent category is TL Cash Loan, and its parent category is Loans. The hierarchy levels can be dynamic and defined by the company.
    - In a telco, postpaid voice plan is a product, and its parent category is Postpaid, and its parent category is Mobile, and its parent category is Voice. 

- Customers: each company has its own set of customers. The customer info mainly contains  demographics, product ownership & usage, transactions, and support tickets. There are also some analytical relations between customers and products or transactions to summarize the customer behavior and performance measurement.

- Sales Team: each company has its own sales team. The sales team is organized in a hierarchical structure. LOBs, Regions, Teams/Branches, Branch Managers and Employees, etc. The structure should be flexible and defined by the company. 

- Performance: each company has its own set of performance metrics. The performance metrics are defined based on the company type and industry. There are some form of targetting mechanism for each company sales employee. A performance metric can be defined for a customer, a product, or a combination of both. For example, a bank may set a target for its sales team to increase the number of loans to customers who have a high credit score and a good transaction history. Or a customer activity level can be a performance metric for a sales employee. Each company may define its own metrics and targets. 

- Analytics: There are analytic models for customer behavior, product tendency and churn prediction. These models are used to generate customer based insights for the sales team. 

- Actions: Actions are suggestions for the sales team to take based on the insights generated by the analytic models, rule based insights, or manual inputs from the sales team. Not necessarily be related to a product or transaction, an action represents any type of activity that the sales team can take to improve the customer relationship or increase the sales. For example, a bank may suggest to its sales team to offer a loan to a customer who has a high credit score and a good transaction history. Or just to call a customer that has not been contacted for a while, or exposes low activity. Each action may have a kind of dependency on other actions or insights. This dependency can be used to generate a que of actions when any of these actions are plenned by the sales manager. For example, a sales manager may want to sell a new product to a customer who has recently contacted with the sales rep. Before closing the sale, the rep should have a meeting with the customer. So the meeting action should be planned after the sale action. and after sales process there may be steps to inform the delivery department, billing department, etc. These steps can be represented as set of actions with dependency to the sales action. Each step will be an action itself and each action will able to agentically communicate with the core system to take the required actions on behalf of the rep (where the company has the capability). 

- Briefings: each sales representative will be able to read daily/weekly/monthly briefings about their customers, products, and actions or the overall economy or the company, etc. Briefings are generated by the AI and are updated regularly. 

- Product Insights: sales representatives will be able to read AI generated insights about their product based metrics, mostly based on improving their performance on the products.

## Relations Between Entities:
- Company has customers, sales employees, products, performance metrics, analytics models, and actions. Every entity is owned by a tenant company.
- Customers can have multiple products, each having a start date, end date, and some metrics.
- Customers product usage metrics are aggregated data based on a time window. For example, a customer may have a monthly usage metric for a product.
- Customers can have multiple transactions, each having a date, amount, type, etc. Transactions are aggregated data from the company's core systems, not raw data.
- Each sales employee is assigned to a set of customers. This assignment can be done in multiple ways. Directly, or through a team/branch structure. They are also hierarchically assigned to a manager, branch, region, etc. The sales team structure is also defined by the company.
- Performance metrics are defined by the company and can be assigned to customers, products, or a combination of both. A product usage volume, duration, experience score, etc. A metric may be calculated from different aspects, for example, a bank may define a metric for a TL Cash Loans by its stock volume, flow volume, number of customers owning, number of new customers, etc. These metrics can be aggregated at different levels, for example, at a branch level, or at a region level, or at a company level and may change from company to company. The database should be flexible enough to accommodate these changes. 
- Analytics models are defined by the company and can be assigned to customers, products, or a combination of both. Usually, model results will be delived as customer_id, product_id, reason (if any), score, date, etc. These results are stored in the database and will be used to generate actions.
- Actions are defined by the company and can be assigned to customers, products, or a combination of both. 

## Features:
- When a sales rep logs in, they will see a briefing. This briefing will be retrieved from the database where an agentic system feeds. So keep in mind that there will be certain parts where the same content will be consumed by multiple users. Since this part will be modular, make the database design flexible enough to support this. 

- A sales rep will start a multi-step action plan for a customer. So there should be a predefined action types and a mapping of dependent action types for each company. And the company should define these using the system. And this brings the necessity of a action definer and an action type approver roles. Keep this relation in mind for similar features (like products, metrics, analytics models, etc.).

- Logging and audability is a must. Every action, every change, every update should be logged and auditable. This is especially important for the actions, as they will be used to generate insights and recommendations for the sales team. 

- Action status can be defined as pending, in_progress, completed, cancelled. But the company may add/remove/modify these statuses. So the database should be flexible enough to accommodate these tenant based changes. Keep in mind that every featue should be designed tenant structure in mind.

- Each action will be tracked for its completion status. Consider adding a scheduling structure for tracking the action results. What to track should also be defined by the company, which also requires a definer and approver role structure and a change management architecture. Keep in mind that change management is a must.

- For AI capable companies, some actions will have automated execution capabilities. For example, a sales rep can schedule a meeting with a customer, and the system will automatically send an email to the customer to confirm the meeting. Or a sales rep can schedule a call with a customer, and the system will automatically call the customer to confirm the call. These automated actions should be logged and auditable. And the company should be able to define these automated actions using the system. And this brings the necessity of a action definer and an action type approver roles. Since the automated actions triggers and execution steps will change from company to company, and even from time to time for the same company, the database should be flexible enough to accommodate these changes.
